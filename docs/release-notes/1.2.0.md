### What's changed

PodBridge now recognises when Windows loses all your audio devices and shows you
how to get them back.

#### Added

- **Recovery guidance when Windows drops every audio device.** After a long
  sleep/resume — especially with several apps playing sound and then joining a
  call (e.g. Teams) — Windows' own audio service can fail to re-enumerate your
  sound devices, so every output and input disappears at once (even wired devices
  PodBridge never touches, like line-out). PodBridge now detects this and shows a
  notification plus an in-app recovery guide: restart the PC, or restart the
  *Windows Audio* and *Windows Audio Endpoint Builder* services (with a button
  that opens Services for you), and a tip to fully shut down rather than sleep
  between long idle periods. This is a Windows-level failure, **not** a PodBridge
  bug — PodBridge cannot fix it, only guide you — and it never elevates or
  restarts a service itself. Still driver-free, no admin.
  ([#173](https://github.com/bhemsen/PodBridge/issues/173))

#### Docs

- Documented the Windows audio-stack collapse as a known limitation in the
  [user guide](https://github.com/bhemsen/PodBridge/blob/main/docs/user/README.md#troubleshooting)
  ([#174](https://github.com/bhemsen/PodBridge/pull/174)), and added Mermaid
  [architecture diagrams](https://github.com/bhemsen/PodBridge/blob/main/docs/architecture-diagrams.md)
  for contributors ([#176](https://github.com/bhemsen/PodBridge/pull/176)).

Every default (Tier-1) feature keeps working with no driver and no admin.
