### What's changed

A maintenance release: the driver-free microphone-profile policy is more robust,
and dependencies plus CI actions are refreshed. Every default (Tier-1) feature
keeps working with no driver and no admin.

#### Fixed

- **Media audio now reliably stays on the high-quality (AAC/A2DP) endpoint.**
  Real AirPods expose both an A2DP "Headphones" and an HFP "Headset" render
  endpoint sharing one device, so the microphone-profile policy could bind media
  playback to the mono, call-quality (HFP) endpoint depending on the order
  Windows enumerated them. It now deterministically prefers the A2DP endpoint, so
  playback keeps its full-quality stereo output. The policy-rollback path was also
  made idempotent, so a persistent audio-configuration failure can no longer cause
  an apply↔rollback loop.
  ([#151](https://github.com/bhemsen/PodBridge/pull/151))

#### Changed

- Dependency and CI-action maintenance, with no effect on the app's behaviour:
  bumped `actions/setup-dotnet`, `actions/attest-build-provenance`,
  `github/codeql-action`, and the test-only `Microsoft.Extensions.TimeProvider.Testing`,
  and re-pinned the deferred GitHub-Actions major bumps by commit SHA. The
  advanced-tier driver-release workflow now always emits its `checksums.sha256`.
